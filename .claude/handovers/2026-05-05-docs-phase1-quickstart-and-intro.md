## README quick-start (`d53b96e`) — long iteration, design notes

This took most of the session. Quick-start is supposed to be ~6 lines
that "smoke-test the package." pyRVtest's irreducible setup (data,
demand, models, cost shifters, test instruments) made that hard — the
naive demo wouldn't show anything sharp.

### What got committed

- **`pyRVtest/data/example_synthetic.csv`** (new, 484 KB): T=3000
  duopoly, single-product firms, generated under **perfect-competition
  truth** with logit demand. Columns: `market_ids, firm_ids, shares,
  prices, x1, z1, z2, rival_z1, rival_z2`. Generated by
  `simulate_example(T=3000, seed=42)` — values in CSV match the
  function's output to 8 sig-fig precision.
- **`pyRVtest.data.load_example()`** — fast path, reads the shipped
  CSV.
- **`pyRVtest.data.simulate_example(T, seed)`** — generates the data
  programmatically. Documented DGP in the docstring (PC truth, logit
  demand, parameter values, share / cost equations). Per sklearn's
  `load_*` / `make_*` convention, having a public sibling function
  makes the simulation transparent — users can vary T or seed, or
  inspect `inspect.getsource()` to see what the demo data is.
- **`README.rst` Quick start section** — 6-line code block, verbatim
  ProblemResults output table, narrative reading of the output.

### How the demo reads

Models 0-3 = Bertrand / Cournot / Monopoly / Perfect Competition. Truth
is PC. The output table shows:

- **Bertrand and Monopoly: cleanly rejected** — MCS p-values 0.0,
  every TRV involving them is *** at 1%, F-stats †††^^^.
- **PC: best fit** — MCS p-value 1.0.
- **Cournot: surviving the MCS at p = 0.675** despite not being the
  truth. The TRV(Cournot, PC) cell is the only insignificant
  comparison (0.419), and its F-stat is 0.0 †††. **This is the
  Dearing degeneracy result, visible in the output:** under logit
  demand, both Cournot and PC have diagonal pass-through matrices, so
  rival cost shifters cannot distinguish them.

The README narrative explicitly walks the reader through this.

### The DGP exploration that landed me here

The path to this clean output was not obvious. Chris pushed back hard
on intermediate setups, and the back-and-forth uncovered real
identification points:

1. **First instinct: Bertrand-truth multi-product (3 firms × 2
   products) at T=200.** Bertrand-vs-Cournot and Bertrand-vs-Monopoly
   were sharp, but Bertrand-vs-PC was weak (TRV ≈ -0.4). Couldn't get
   it to budge by varying T, alpha, beta_0 in multi-product.

2. **Diagnosed why:** under multi-product logit Bertrand,
   per-product shares are small (~0.1), so cross-elasticity α·s_j·s_k
   is small (~0.024). Bertrand's off-diagonal pass-through scales
   with cross-elasticity, so rival cost shifters carry weak signal
   about the true Bertrand markup → PC's GMM moment is small → weak
   test.

3. **Fixed by collapsing to duopoly single-product**: per-product
   share ~0.45, cross-elasticity ~0.20 (10× bigger), Bertrand-vs-PC
   becomes sharp at T=3000 (TRV = -4.22 ***).

4. **Chris's pushback re Cournot vs PC**: under logit, both Cournot
   and PC have diagonal pass-through (zero off-diagonal), so per
   DMQSW's identification result, rival cost shifters cannot
   distinguish them. I had been showing TRV(Cournot, PC) as a
   significant cell under Bertrand truth — defensible (both are
   misspecified candidates at Bertrand-equilibrium shares, the
   Cournot formula amplifies share variation differently than PC's
   zero), but the comparison wasn't pedagogically what we wanted.

5. **Chris's resolution**: switch the truth to **PC** (or Cournot).
   Then under PC truth: Bertrand and Monopoly genuinely have
   different pass-through ratios (positive, negative) → cleanly
   rejected. Cournot has matching diagonal → genuinely degenerate
   against PC → MCS keeps both. **This is the Dearing degeneracy
   demonstrated empirically.**

6. **PC truth is also free of pyblp dependency** — prices = mc + ω,
   no equilibrium fixed point, just compute logit shares from those
   prices. The quick-start is now pure pyRVtest, no `import pyblp`.

7. **Sanity check**: pyblp's logit estimator on the shipped data
   recovers β = [2.015, -0.999, 0.996] vs DGP truth [2.0, -1.0, 1.0]
   — to within standard errors. Confirms the DGP math is consistent
   with what pyblp would compute under PC.

### One quirk noted, not investigated

In the output table, every F-stat cell involving model 3 (PC) shows
F = 0.0, 2.6, or 1.4 — all very low — even though some pairwise TRVs
involving PC are sharply significant. This appears to be a
pseudo-degeneracy in the F-stat formula when one model has identically
zero markup (residual variance collapses for that model, the
sigma-cross terms collapse, and the F denominator structure
degenerates). Not blocking the demo — the TRV statistics are
well-defined and the MCS p-values are sensible — but worth a future
look at `pyRVtest/solve/test_engine.py` F-stat computation under
identically-zero-markup models. **Tracked here as a v0.5 follow-up
candidate.**

## Notable surprises / discoveries

1. **The DMQSS paper title was made up last session** — handover
   documented this honestly, this session fixed it. Real title is
   "Conduct and Scale Economies: Evaluating Tariffs in the US
   Automobile Market," and the paper applies its methodology to the US
   auto market with tariffs (despite being the methodology source for
   `endogenous_cost_component` support in the package).
2. **pyblp 1.1.0 is broken on numpy 2.3.4** — `np.unicode_` was
   removed in NumPy 2.0 but pyblp 1.1.0 still uses it. CI matrix pins
   the right combos so this isn't a pyRVtest issue, but it's a
   tripwire for anyone trying to run notebooks locally on a recent
   numpy.
3. **Multi-product Bertrand has weak Bertrand-vs-PC test power
   under logit.** Diagnosed via Q[PC]−Q[Bertrand] not growing with N
   (population moment ~0). Root cause: cross-elasticity α·s_j·s_k
   is tiny when per-product shares are small. Fix: collapse to
   duopoly. Worth knowing for anyone designing similar demos.
4. **Dearing degeneracy is empirically clean under PC truth.** With
   PC truth + 4 candidate models (Bertrand, Cournot, Monopoly, PC),
   the TRV(Cournot, PC) cell shows F=0.0 and TRV=0.42 (insignificant)
   — the degeneracy is **visible in the output**, not just predicted
   theoretically. This is a strong pedagogical demonstration.
5. **`load_example` + `simulate_example` is the sklearn convention.**
   Shipping the CSV as a fast path and the simulation as a public
   function (sibling to `make_classification`) addresses the "is this
   data cherry-picked" concern transparently.
6. **F-stat anomaly under zero markup** (item 1 in v0.5 follow-up
   candidates above) — flagged but not investigated. Could be a real
   bug in the F-stat denominator computation, or a known limitation
   that should be documented as such.
