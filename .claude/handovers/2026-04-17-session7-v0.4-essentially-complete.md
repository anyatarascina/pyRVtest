## Honest retrospective

### Wins

- Full v0.4 library scope landed in one day, including the Dearing paper integration (which wasn't on the morning's plan).
- Parallel-worktree pattern continued to pay off: 3 rounds × 3 agents + several solo agents, all integrated cleanly with only three small merge conflicts, all resolvable in <2 minutes each.
- Snapshot safety rail held — every refactor was bit-identical at `atol=1e-10`. That's the strongest argument that the math is still correct after the `Problem.solve` split and the tax machinery changes.
- Chris's design sharpening was decisive several times: rule-of-thumb already exists via `cost_scaling` (I missed that); problem-level taxes + salience opt-out is cleaner than my first "per-model dict" proposal; HHI is endogenous in labor markets.

### Losses / errors worth recording

- **Misread the `cost_scaling` machinery** at step 12 start — assumed rule-of-thumb needed a new `_compute_markup(p)` API. Chris corrected with a pointer to the existing docs. Cost: ~10 minutes of wrong direction.
- **Misread token-quota vs context-window percentages.** Early in the session, when Chris said "at 91%" and "at 96%", I read those as context-window fill (and was overly cautious about stopping) when he meant session-quota consumption. He had to prompt "content window only at 8%" before I realized and agreed to launch session 7 in-place. Lesson: ask explicitly which percentage when a number is reported without units.
- **`.gitignore` append bug.** First attempt at `echo ".claude/worktrees/" >> .gitignore` merged onto the `venv` line (`venv.claude/worktrees/`) silently, making the pattern useless. Caught two sessions later when worktrees leaked into a `git add -A` commit. Fixed in `b0aae79`. Lesson: verify `.gitignore` with a trailing `git check-ignore` after any programmatic append.
- **Agent 14 shipped `concentration_hhi` on labor instruments** without flagging it as a theoretical concern. Chris caught the endogeneity issue in review. Lesson to pass to future agent briefs for labor work: explicitly note that labor concentration is endogenous in the wage-setting DGP.
- **Accidentally committed agent worktrees as submodule refs twice** (via `git add -A` when `.gitignore` wasn't yet working). Cleaned both times, second fix permanent.

### Open confidence uncertainty

- Agent 14's labor sign convention pairwise consistency is verified (`Bertrand(D)` magnitude ≡ `BertrandWages(-D)` magnitude) but the absolute sign convention against a specific labor paper is not — flagged for Marco/Lorenzo.
- Agent 24 (OQ 15 PT diagnostics) flagged: `offdiag_frobenius` implementation takes the Frobenius norm of `D - diag(diag(D))` where `D = P_i - P_j`. High confidence this matches Dearing Remark 4's literal reading, but worth a paper cross-check if an external reviewer looks at the diagnostic output.
